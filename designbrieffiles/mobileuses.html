Uses of UAS Technology

Today, drones serve multiple purposes for its user. Businesses and the public both benefit from the use of drones. Drones are useful tools of technology that can complete tasks faster than ever before.

**For simplicity reasons, the term "drones" will be used instead of Unmanned Aerial Systems.

Commercial and

Personal Uses

Some real estate companies are using drones to help them get an immense look at their properties. They use drones to capture aerial photos of properties for sale. This does not only help the realtor but the customer too. Customers can then use these videos or images to grasp what they are buying from a different angle.

Aircraft companies and maintenance businesses use drones to perform inspections on large aircraft. This enables them to quickly examine the entire aircraft and analyze issues. This allows them to find problems quickly without having to take the time to manually navigate to the designated area on the exterior of the plane.

One of the most critical facilities that use drone technologies is a search and rescue team. The team will use a drone when a large helicopter is not safe to navigate around small or dangerous areas. For example, if a hiker is in a forest and found theirself in a dangerous situation and needed to be rescued, the search and rescue team could use a drone to get a better view and locate the hiker quickly.

Extreme athletes and TV broadcasting networks use drones to capture footage when static cameras and helicopters are obsolete and useless when following athletes around a course. Drones allow them to get closer and take clearer pictures while not getting in the way.
