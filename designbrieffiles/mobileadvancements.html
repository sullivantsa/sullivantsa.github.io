Future Advancements for UAS Technology

Drones of tomorrow should be better built so they can be used for all sorts of tasks. They could even be required to endure the most dangerous expeditions.

**For simplicity reasons, the term "drones" will be used instead of Unmanned Aerial Systems.

Advancements

Sustaining a crash is an important aspect for drone users. If engineers innovated the drone to withstand crashes, users wouldn't need to be experts on navigating these machines. Although it is an important lesson for users to learn, being able to control a drone in extremely windy conditions shouldn't be only the users concern. The manufacturers inability to create sturdy machines is not the users fault.

Implementing the use of sensors in drones to avert obstacles would be a remarkable improvement to avoid completely destroying the drone from a single crash. Most mediocre drones are inexpensively built and do not have measures to prevent crashing. So far, there are numerous accidents a year involving the use of drones and improper use.

Drones overtime could be downsized to fit into cramped spaces and smaller areas, such as caves and caverns. Downsizing drones would allow photographers to capture pulchritudinous photographs.
