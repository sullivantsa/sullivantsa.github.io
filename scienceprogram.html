Technology & Engineering Program

Our school has a multitude of Technology and Engineering classes that will allow students to excel in what they do best. Currently, we have the following classes that correspond to the previous criteria:

Intro to Engineering and Design
Principles of Engineering
Computer Applications
Computer Programming
Multimedia (Graphic Design)
Multimedia (Video Animation)

Summaries of these Classes

Introduction to Engineering and Design

Students will explore the history of engineering along with other career opportunities within the field. Students will learn the design process, sketching and visualization techniques, geometric relationships, as well as modeling and assembly modeling, analysis, verification and model documentation. Models of product solutions are created, analyzed and communicated using solid modeling computer design. This course teaches problem­solving skills using a design development process and involves hands­on learning along with intense use of the following computer applications: Microsoft Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Autodesk Inventor.

Contact Ms. Ritchie (ritchiej@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.

Principles of Engineering

Through problems that engage and challenge, students explore a broad range of engineering topics including mechanisms, the strength of structures and materials, and automation. Students develop skills in problem solving, research, and design while learning strategies for design process documentation, collaboration, and presentation. This survey course of engineering exposes students to some of the major concepts they’ll encounter in a postsecondary engineering course of study. Students have an opportunity to investigate engineering and high­tech careers and to develop skills and understanding of engineering concepts.

Contact Ms. Ritchie (ritchiej@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.

Computer Applications

This course enables students to become proficient in using the programs from the Microsoft Office Suite for computers. Emphasis will be given to e­mail, word processing, desktop publishing, spreadsheets, presentation software and database software. The purpose of this course is to prepare students to use software that has become standard in most places of business. Completion of this course.

Contact Ms. Paneitz (paneitzh@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.

Computer Programming

This is an entry­level course into the field of Computer Science. It is an introduction into the development of computer programming techniques using the Java language, C#, and Visual Basic. Emphasis will be placed on learning and applying good object­oriented programming techniques.

Contact Ms. Boyer (boyers@sullivaneagles.org) or Ms. Payne (paynej@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.

Multimedia (Graphic Design)

Use and development of multimedia ­ combining text, graphics, sound, still and video images and animation into a unified, seamless document. Examines the nature of the various media, the capture or creation, digitalization and modification of each media type, the architecture and technology of multimedia systems, the principles behind effective multimedia presentations, analysis and design of systems and multimedia development through the use of common software packages.

Contact Ms. Boyer (boyers@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.

Multimedia (Video Animation)

This course covers advanced website design, flash animations, video editing, kinetic typography, theory and practice of advanced multimedia applications. This course utilizes various software applications such as Adobe CS3, Flash, Dreamweaver, Expression Web.

Contact Ms. Boyer (boyers@sullivaneagles.org) for more information.
